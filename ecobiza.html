I needed to be careful while protecting myself.

Rhey Ecobiza

ER Nurse

I remember it was around the second week of March when the enhanced community quarantine (ECQ) started. It had a huge impact on the way we lived, the way we worked. (Of course, it’s the first time any of us had to deal with a pandemic). At first, we still didn’t feel its effect, this pandemic and the ECQ, there was just less traffic. But it was still quite challenging as we had to change living arrangements – we couldn’t go home, we already had to stay in dormitories. At least there was wifi, and we could still video call our families. I just got married last January; it was quite tough, my wife and I not being together.
