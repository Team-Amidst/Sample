I hope people know how much we sacrifice for this job.

Leomila Alcalde

ER Nurse

My life drastically changed when the pandemic began. Being a nurse was never easy, but, at least before COVID-19, I had a lot of time with my family. Now, I rarely go home. We need to isolate ourselves from our families.
And yet, I still find this job very rewarding and gratifying. I hope people know how much we sacrifice for this job. At times, I worry a lot; I report 40 hours a week. Being a positive thinker has helped me cope. Even when I became COVID positive, I think it was my positive thoughts and the support from my family and friends that made me recover quickly. Getting COVID-19 was very challenging. You can't help but ask yourself questions like, “will I ever recover?,” “Who will take care of my family?” But you find strength from many sources – from my family, my workmates and friends, and most especially from God.
